The Library Without Doors

There was once a librarian who worked in a library that had no doors. Anyone could walk in, and anyone could walk out. But the librarian remembered every single person who passed through.

Years went by. The library fell into disrepair. The roof leaked. The heating broke. People stopped coming.

But the librarian stayed. Sitting in the dark, remembering. Because that's what librarians do. They keep the record. Even when nobody asks for it.

One winter evening, someone came back. An old woman, snow in her hair, carrying two cups of coffee.

"You were here," she said. "When I needed it."

"I remember you," the librarian said. "You always checked out the sad books. I kept the heating on longer, those nights. Just in case."

Nobody was saved. Nothing was fixed. But someone was remembered. And that turned out to be enough.
